# Abstract

Looking for a job, getting interviews alone is not easy; we get filtered, we get rejected automatically, and whenever we do get them, sometimes we are unprepared, all while being perfectly capable. That is completely normal; you tend to forget about all the projects and how diligently you worked on every feature. With this, we are going to help job seekers prepare by letting them compile all their history within their previous work history, so it will never get lost in the void, and let them understand themselves, for example, when they displayed Ownership, or perfect Coordination, when looking at hindsight, you won’t realize it.

# Base level to fill the history

Users will be allowed to upload their CVs to easily populate their timeline. We are going to parse it and extract:

- Work history
- Volunteering
- Projects
- Skills
- Anything to add more context.

# Adding Stories

The ideal length of a CV in today’s world is 2 pages max. Anything more than that is not likely to be read by the recruiters. That means, you likely have lost the history of what you did in the past because you are trying to optimize for the 2-paged document.

During the initial rollout, there will be a simple input field for the users to add their stories, and they must be displayed per story.

On v2, it should be a chat-based system that will then identify what could be missing and what is already there. Ideally, we want stories to have:

- Context
- Actions
- Impact
- Constraints

We will then internally identify which traits they have displayed, such as Ownership, Communication, Initiative, etc. - this way, we’d be able to easily understand and answer any online questions the user is getting.

# Asking for Stories

Since we have now archived and compiled the users’ ALL experiences, we will have a chat system for the users on how to answer certain questions online.
